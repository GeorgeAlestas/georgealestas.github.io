A few words about me!

I'm a Greek PhD student, studying at the University of Ioannina with Prof. Leandros Perivolaropoulos as my advisor. This means that I'm currently part of this awesome Cosmology group.

My research, thus far, is focused around the Hubble tension. Without getting too technical this a famous cosmological enigma that has to do with the differences between the values of the expansion rate of the Universe, as they're calculated using different methods. The gist of it, is that all the methods employed should produce the same value give or take, but much to our surprise they DON'T!! Is there a problem with the methodologies used? Is it a hint for new and exciting physics? Who knows? God only knows at the moment and someday I hope you and me as well.

Among others, I'm interested in exploring and analysing cosmological datasets, modified theories of gravity, dark energy, dark matter and solitons. When I'm not doing physics I enjoy watching movies, some tv series, reading books, programming and cooking for the lucky few that can handle it...
